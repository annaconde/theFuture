Types of artificial intelligence

Artificial intelligence falls into two main categories: functionality-based AI and feature-based AI.

Functionality Based

Reactive Machine – This AI has no memory power and no ability to learn from previous actions. IBM's Deep Blue falls into this category. Bounded Theory – With the addition of memory, this AI uses prior information to make better decisions. Common apps like GPS location apps fall into this category. Theory of Mind – This AI is still being developed with the aim of having a very deep understanding of human minds. Self-aware AI – This AI, which can understand and evoke human emotions, in addition to having its own, is still only hypothetical.

Resource Based

Narrow artificial intelligence (ANI) – A system that performs narrowly defined programmed tasks. This AI has a combination of reactive and limited memory. Most current AI apps fall into this category. Artificial General Intelligence (AGI) – This AI is capable of training, learning, understanding and acting like a human. Artificial Super Intelligence (ASI) – This AI performs tasks better than humans due to its superior data processing, memory, and decision-making capabilities. There are currently no real-world examples.
The relationship between artificial intelligence, machine learning and deep learning Artificial intelligence is a branch of computer science that seeks to simulate human intelligence in a machine. AI systems are governed by algorithms using techniques such as machine learning and deep learning to demonstrate “smart” behavior.
